1)appa


Popular in South India and Sri Lanka, appam, also known as hoppers or appam, is a sort of thin pancake shaped like a bowl that is made with a fermented batter made of rice (or rice flour) and coconut milk.

Course: Appetizer, Dessert


4)kirihodi

Popular and traditional in Sri Lanka, kiri hodi is a fragrant gravy made with coconut milk. It is typically served hot with string hoppers or coconut sambol.

Course: Main Course

2)kokis

One of the most often consumed traditional sweets in Sri Lanka is kokis. They are made with rice flour and coconut milk and deep-fried using a decorative flower-shaped mold. They are served specifically during the Avurudu festival.

Course: Dessert

3)indiappa

A classic South Indian and Sri Lankan dish called indiappa is made by pressing rice flour, also known as finger millet flour, into thin noodles. After that, the noodles are steam-cooked and served with coconut milk gravy, also known as coconut sambol.

Course: Main Course, Side Dish
